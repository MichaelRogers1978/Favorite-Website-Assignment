Fun facts about Hiking: Hiking was the inspiration for skiing and snowshoeing. Bears basically spend their entire lives hiking. The word "hiking" likely originated from the English dialectal term "hyke," meaning "to walk vigorously," and is thought to be related to the verb "hitch," which meant "to move, jerk, stir". The activity also can reduce anxiety and depression, plus improve cognition, memory function, and problem-solving skills.

Fun facts about Camping: Camping can teach valuable skills, such as cooking over a campfire, building shelters, and navigating outdoors. 33% of campers always camp with pets. While camping as a recreational activity has roots in various cultures and time periods, Thomas Hiram Holding, a British tailor, is often credited with popularizing modern recreational camping with his 1908 book "The Camper's Handbook".

Fun facts about Fishing: Fishing can be traced back to around 40,000 years ago, and there are over 20,000 known fish species. The first fishing reels were invented in 4th century China. Oldest fish hook that we found until now is made of shell and is between 16,000 and 23,000 years old.
